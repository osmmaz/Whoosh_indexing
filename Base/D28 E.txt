This research is concerned with hedging against rate of return risk in Islamic
banks with special focus on GCC Islamic banks. The research relies on
content analysis of three existing streams of literature on this topic. The first
stream supports the use of conventional derivatives, the second supports
replication of these products and the third discourage the use of both and
suggest other alternatives This research also attempts to find out whether
GCC central banks have issued specific instructions with regards to the risk of
concern to Islamic banks and finally it attempts know what are the rate of
return hedging practices as disclosed in the annual reports of selected banks
from the GCC.
